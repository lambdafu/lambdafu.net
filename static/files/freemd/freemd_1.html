1. Introduction

FreeMD is a project to allow access to net MD compatible devices using Free Software. It comes with a library that allows to add support for Net MD devices to a program. It also includes program to manipulate NetMD devices from the command line.

FreeMD uses libusb to access the device.

1.2 Features

FreeMD has a couple of features that make it interesting for you to use.

it's free software
Anybody can use, modify, and redistribute it under the terms of the GNU General Public License (see section B. GNU GENERAL PUBLIC LICENSE).

it's flexible
FIXME

it's easy
FreeMD hides the differences between the protocol and device access from the programmer and user behind an easy-to-use interface. This way the programmer can focus on the other parts of the program, and still integrate Net MD support in their application. Once support for FreeMD has been added to a program, it is easy to add support for future Net MD compatible devices and features. The user can just concentrate on getting the most out of their hardware.
